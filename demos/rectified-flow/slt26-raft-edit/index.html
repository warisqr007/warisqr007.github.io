RAFT-Edit: Relative Attribute Flow for Static Speaker Trait Editing

Anonymous submission to SLT 26
OverviewAgeGenderPitchHNRGender + Pitch

Abstract

Speech reveals not only linguistic content and speaker identity, but also static traits such as age presentation, gender presentation, pitch, and voice quality, which can support profiling or unwanted inference. Although speaker anonymization aims to reduce such risks, most systems modify the speaker representation as a single identity variable, offering limited control over which traits are changed or preserved. We propose RAFT-Edit, a source-relative rectified-flow editor for controllable static-trait manipulation in normalized speaker-embedding space. Because paired trait-edited speech is unavailable, RAFT-Edit learns from real source-target speaker-embedding pairs using weakly supervised relative attribute rankers. By editing only the speaker embedding while keeping linguistic content fixed, RAFT-Edit isolates trait-level privacy effects. Experiments show monotonic, source-aware edits after synthesis and demonstrate that individual trait controls increase unlinkability while preserving ASR intelligibility.
